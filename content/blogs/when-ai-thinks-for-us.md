It's really fascinating how we went from learning bits, bytes to witnessing the existence of LLM's and AI assistants. 

Technology evolve, so do we! But that said, everything has its own ups and downs.

Hence, I would like to discuss one such part of it - <mark>AI making us lazy</mark>.

On 30 November 2022, OpenAI launched ChatGPT. It was trained on internet data from 2021 which is basically an endless sea of information throughout our existence. 

**Everything about the assistant and its capability of breaking down complex problems changed the world forever.**

Before AI and LLMs many tasks had to be done manually. Like writing essays, content, doing certain calculations, debugging code. And I know you can relate to this as well. 

Ever since usage of AI came into practice, many of these things went automated and many people no longer manually do these things. 

Think about a student who needs to write an essay on "The importance of education". Instead of thinking and writing it manually he simply has an access to AI assistant in his pocket. He simply can make use of it get it done within 5 mins or less. But my question is: even if it's saving his time and making it really fast process, <mark>is it really a good thing?</mark>

Or let's say a random artist needs to generate some art idea. Instead of forcing his mind can't he just use AI and save tons of his time?


## The Good Side of AI 

AI has helped us in ways we couldn't even imagine. It has helped us in making our lives <mark>easier, faster, and more efficient</mark>. 

> **Accelerated Learning:** Instead of spending hours searching the web for concepts, we can now use AI to break down complex mathematical and physical equations instantly.

I still remember my school days to solve any maths or physics problem, I had to scroll through the entire internet to get the concept of how can I solve the particular problem. Of course it used to consume a lot of my time but now I can get it done within 5 mins or less. 

I would simply make use of AI to understand the problem and getting it figured out how to solve it. Instead of solving 3 questions in 2-3 hours, I can solve <mark>10 questions in that time</mark>. AI can really help us in **learning stuff in less time** and being a **truly powerful personal assistant**.


## But here is the down side of it

At the same time people are overly depending on AI. Even or simplest and smallest task they <mark>can't have it done without AI</mark>. 

I still remember one day me and my friends were having a good time and we played "Never have I ever". One of my friend just to ask the questions he was making use of ChatGPT and I was like, "what are you doing?". I felt really concerned not only for him but <mark>the way people are over-depending on such tools</mark>.

Due to overdependence on AI I feel like people are **losing their natural creativity and thinking capability**. And this is becoming a big concern for all of us. 

Think about a child who is growing up and has access to AI. <mark>He will never learn to struggle and solve problems on his own</mark>. He will never learn the value of hard work and perseverance. And he will never learn to think for himself or have his own ideology. 

My concern is that we’re raising a generation that knows how to ask for answers but not always how to discover them.

## My Personal Thoughts

I have nothing against AI infact I use it myself as an assitant while I am building something also in my work life. But the problem arises when people start **over-depending on such tools**.

For example:

Outside of my work/tech life I do arts maybe painting, sketching or digital. I never use AI for any references or even ideas. People often ask me "what is the purpose of doing arts when we have AI to generate it in seconds?" 

Yes, I do agree but that is not the thing. I do arts because it makes my brain <mark>"think"</mark>. It sort of **practices my natural thinking capacity and keep my natural creativity alive**.

Even if I am gaming sometimes, I mostly play games which requires strategic mindset. I think by my brain instead of letting AI assist me prepare my battle deck.

What I am trying to say is we <mark>don't need to depend on AI all the time</mark>. Sometimes we need to **let our natural thinking come out and trust it**. And these are few norms I mentioned which can make us practice it. Maybe making arts, playing strategic based games, reading books etc. 

AI will continue to evolve and we need to adapt to it but at the same time we also need to let our <mark>natural brain thinking power thriving!</mark>
