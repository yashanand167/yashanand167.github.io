# What Bangalore Taught Me

Four years ago, I came to Bangalore with a simple plan: finish college, grab a degree and go back home.

Initially I thought this city is just an another chapter in my life, something temporary. I thought post college I'll return back to Kathmandu and continue my life from there.

But somewhere in between Bangalore became more than a place for me. It eventually became the place where **I actually grew up**.

Back in my school days in Kathmandu, there were many moments where things were brutally unfair to me. There were situations where I wanted to speak up, fight back, or change things, but I didn’t know how. I felt limited by my own confidence and circumstances and the environment that surrounded me.

Looking back at those days, I realised this is not the version of me I am meant for.

Staying here in Bangalore, this city taught me a very important life lesson: **"nobody is coming to build the future for you"**

At first, that realization felt uncomfortable. But slowly, it became empowering. I understood that if I wanted something, **I had to take action**. If I wanted to grow, I had to put myself in uncomfortable situations. If I wanted a different life, **I had to create it myself**.

Bangalore **made me independent**.

But this city wasn't the only thing that changed me.

College introduced me to people who saw something in me that I hadn’t fully recognized myself. I met people who understood my potential, challenged my thinking, and pushed me beyond the limits I had created for myself.

They didn't just become friends; they **became part of my growth**

**Being surrounded by ambitious people changes the way you look at yourself.** When you see people around you constantly learning, creating, and improving, you naturally start questioning your own limits.

They pushed me to aim higher. To take myself more seriously. To believe that I could build something meaningful.

## The Shift of Mindset

Post school, people who tried to limit me and my mindset started disappearing from my life. I cut them off completely without any regrets to this day. 

I surrounded myself with people who had that **builder mindset**, one who are more intellectual than me

When I first arrived, I never imagined that four years later I'll only get to visit home twice. The strange part is, it wasn’t because I stopped caring. It was because somewhere along the way, my priorities changed.

Initially, I did miss home. I missed the comfort, the familiarity, and the feeling of being around people who already knew me.

But after a point, something shifted

The truth is, I don’t think about going back the way I used to.

My mind is occupied with something else now: **building**.

Building a life I never even realized I wanted.

A life where I challenge myself, create things, grow, and become someone I can be proud of.

Bangalore has that energy everywhere.

**Walk into almost any cafe**, you'll find people build their own product or brand or their own startup. Someone working on AI, someone planning their next move. People don't like settling here for less.

This city constantly reminds me everyday that there's a lot to achieve.

Being surrounded by people who are thinking about the future changes you. You start asking yourself, why am I wasting my time?

The next decade of my life is **not about comfort, and definitely not about relationships**. It is about **discipline, growth, and becoming the person I know I can become**.

This city did not make everything easy. In fact, it did the opposite. It challenged me in every step. It forced me to become responsible for myself.

But that challenge shaped me.

The person who came to Bangalore four years ago and the person writing this today are not the same.

I came here thinking I was just moving cities.

I didn’t realize I was **rebuilding myself**.
