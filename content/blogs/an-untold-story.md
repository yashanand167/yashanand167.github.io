## A Journey I Never Planned

The day was 20th September 2022, I left my hometown (Kathmandu, Nepal) for the first time for my undergraduate studies in Bangalore, India. I was pretty uncertain about what I was gonna do. I had never explored tech my life and had absolute zero knowledge on Software Engineering Field.

I have been an artist since my childhood and I always knew that I wanted to pursue a career in the creative field. But I was not sure about what to do. But my parents told me "You are always free to take on your passion. But at one point
you also need a degree right?". I agreed, they asked me to pass exams somehow and grab a degree and rest I can work on my passion. So, I agreed to go for Computer Science and Engineering not for exploring tech but just for degree. 

Almost a  month later, on 16th October 2022, I stepped in my college for the first time, booked my hostel room. I was pretty numb and scared and I didn't know anyone there, new place new life. I couldn't even sleep that night. Next day I went to college to attend my first class and the day was normal. I met new people, made new friends told them about my art life. Everybody had the same question "What are you here for. You must go and pursue your passion." I replied, "I know but I just need degree and rest I will work on my passion". That being said I simply carried my ongoing artwork and called it a day. I absolutely didn't face any pressure as I had the mindset that I am just here for the sake of degree. I didn't even had a laptop and frequently had to borrow it from seniors for giving my exams since they had given us a personalized LMS.

Days passed on, but one day I met a guy who was my new classmate. He had just returned from his hometown and we just met each other. The guy has been into tech since his school days and he showed me a website he built for class to have a free access of resources and materials for competitive exams. I looked at the website and I went stunned. I asked "Did you build this by yourself?" He replied "Yes, I did". I asked out of curiosity "How did you do this?". He said he used JavaScript and certain techstack for all this. I asked "What's JavaScript?". He was surprised realizing I absolutely had no idea about programming. He walked me through his tech stacks and everything and everytime I stayed over in his room just because I was curious. Then I asked "With coding, we can build all this?" He replied "Yes bro". That was when it all started.

What got me into coding was CSS, which is used for styling websites. Since I was an artist and I was always been fascinated by colors I thought maybe give it a try. I asked how I can try it out. He said "Download Visual Studio Code, and I will guide you through the basics". I told my brother I need a laptop since I want to try it and college exams requires it too. I got the laptop somehow and took his help to install everything needed. I followed tutorials on what CSS without even understanding core structure of a web platform. When I saw my first button I made using css and html I went like "Woah, this is something". I asked my friend this is how am I doing it? He asked me to learn the fundamentals and everything of how a website works and he taught me about servers and IP on which my button has been rendered. I was stunned again and then I went full throttle on learning CSS and styling more than the main language. 

First Term Ended and we had C programming subject. I had a hard time understanding syntaxes and logic and at one point I almost gave up on programming entirely. But my friend kept helping me get through the concepts and everything and somehow I coped up. But looking at what he keeps building I knew I can learn a lot from him. I visited my brother's house in Banaglore taking few days off college and showed him what am I upto. Looking at my interest in arts and trying CSS throughout he suggested me to explore product design(UI/UX). I was unaware about the term UI and UX and he left me wondering and he wanted me to figure out by myself.

Soon when I went back to college a hackathon was about to be organized and they required illustrators. Since I had an background in art I went and gave my name to the organizer. He gave me illustration work and I gave my everything into it. I saw people in my team coding day and night while the other guy was involved designing interfaces and I talked to him. That's where I was introduced to the software called "Figma". I saw those beautiful designs and colors being used and I could not stop myself from checking it out. I searched on what Figma is and what UI/UX is. I scrolled through the internet day and night just to grasp the term and why is this role different in sectors. First Semester Ended and I realized I officially have interest beyond arts or just taking a degree. Somehow after that I started exploring things more, it felt like an endless sea at some point. It even led to exhaustion and constant thoughts like "There is so much to learn and know" Beside Figma, I also started learning JavaScript and had my friend beside me to guide on my errors and syntax issues. I had no idea on how web browser works and he used to walk me through everything I must know.
Learning just basics the first year officially ended. 

When I went back home I couldn't rest having the FOMO that people around me know so much in tech, I gotta catch up. Somehow I stepped into frontend with raw HTML, CSS and JavaScript while my peers have already started grinding Backend and API's. I didn't even know what API is and what does it even stand for. Somehow I kept learning Figma and JavaScript simuntaneously but ended up nowhere trying to do multiple things at once. Then my brother asked me to focus on one thing and do it really well. I decided to go all in for UI/UX design and product exploration. I started learning from the fundamentals of design, color theory, typography, composition and everything in between. I tried to design some UI thinking I have the creativity since I am an artist and the UI turned out horrible. I got to know that Product Design and Art are totally different fields. Then I realized, I kept trying failed a lot of times but got the grasp on how a product is designed and handed to developers end-to-end. This took me almost entire 2nd year of my college but due to constant peer pressure and fear of rooting into different path leaving coding for months, I came back to it. I started learning a backend framework to understand how data is communicated and received at client side in frontend. As always my friend helped me out and helped me solving countless errors and bugs I had made. Learning REST API's was fun but challenging and that's how my 2nd year came to an end. 

Starting of 3rd year I made a huge mistake. Instead of priotizing my learning and building, I started chasing internship certificates like my peers and this led me nowhere and I completely wasted like 2 months of my time. I almost did nothing on my 5th semester but learned about how to use Github and what Open Source is. As always it was my friend who helped me learn this. My parents and my brother had absolutely no idea that I am totally invested in tech and software beside my passion for design. Somehow my 5th sem came to an end and 6th sem started. It was a turning point for me as I landed my official first gig for UI/UX design where I had to design an AI powered nutrition app. But at the same time from product thinking to thinking about code logic and at the same time thinking about the market gaps, I had to figure it on my own. Yeah it was challenging but I did it, I gave my everything and they loved the designs. Although I literally took 4 months but it was worth it. But at the same time I was worried that I don't even have a single internship so far so I started chasing one just for resume polish. 

But instead of an internship, me and two other people who belongs to the same college I did, we landed in a startup (Dseide Healthcare Network). The most crazy part about it was we were about to design and code a product from scratch and it was just three of us who had to accomplish it. We took it as a challenge, and we started brainstroming about the product for a month. We did the product design successfully but here came the most challening part for me. Coding the UI's I have designed, I had never touched a frontend framework throughout my 3 years of college and I had to do it in React Native. Somehow I coped up did my work with anti-patterns of frontend ofc which I came to realize later on.  I learned about UI optimization, API integration, data flow, state management, component based architecture and everything in between. It was a rollercoaster of emotions, frustrations, and triumphs. But at the end of the day, I was proud of what we had built. The startup experience was crazy and it really helped me learn a lot and I was a part of this really energetic and "never give up" attitude team members. I was really proud of what we had built but at the same time I knew I have to move on since it was my final year of college and I need to look for better opportunities. 

I tried, I failed countless times but the journey was different and unexpected somehow for a guy like me from being a full time artist to getting into tech and design. 


## What's Next?

I still don't know fully if I belong to one label.

Am I an artist? Am I a designer? Am I a developer? Honestly, I don't know. But one thing I know for sure is that I love creating things and I will continue to do so. I'll keep exploring, keep learning, and keep creating. The journey is far from over, and I am excited to see where it takes me.

I liked colors and interfaces, but I also wanted to understand what happens behind the screen. I liked thinking about users, but I also wanted to know how data flows from one place to another. I wanted to build things from scratch and not just imagine them.

Somewhere along the journey, I stopped seeing this as confusion.

I started seeing it as curiosity.

Right now I am building products, improving my frontend skills, exploring design engineering, and trying to bridge the gap between design and development. There is still a lot that I don't know, and honestly that feeling never really goes away.

But if there is one thing I learned from these past few years, it's that I don't need to have every answer figured out.

I just need to keep building.

Because a guy who once asked, "What's JavaScript?" somehow ended up here.

Maybe this story was never about tech.

Maybe it was about finding something I never expected to love.
