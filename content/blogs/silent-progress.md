## The Society

Society has a weird habit of **judging, comparing, and commenting** on our lives. 

- **People are quick to compare.**
- **Quick to criticize.**
- **Quick to point out where someone is lacking.**

It is easy to sit on the sidelines and analyze someone else’s journey. It is easy to complain about someone’s choices, question someone’s progress, or measure someone’s life against a standard they never chose.

But what is often ignored is **everything happening behind the scenes**:

- **The struggles.**
- **The discipline.**
- **The failures.**
- **The effort nobody applauds.**

> **Many people judge the building because they only see the construction, not the vision.**

They see the current version and assume they know the entire story.

---

## Personal Experience

I have experienced this pattern many times in my own life.

Whenever I achieved something whether it was **winning trophies**, **earning recognition**, or **securing a scholarship**, there were always people who reduced it to a simple statement:

> **"Anyone can do that."**

Maybe anyone can.

But **not everyone does**.

- **Not everyone puts in the hours.**
- **Not everyone stays consistent.**
- **Not everyone keeps showing up when nobody is watching.**

The strange part is, **when some people cannot question the result, they start questioning the journey.**

Even the things I do outside academics became something people had opinions about.

Being an **artist** in my free time was seen as something unusual. Instead of seeing **passion, creativity, or discipline**, some people only saw something to criticize.

And sometimes the comparisons didn’t stop with me. They reached my parents:

> *"My child did this."*
> *"My child achieved that."*

As if someone else’s journey needs to be used as a **weapon against another person’s growth**.

But here is what people fail to understand:

> **You cannot bring someone down by comparing them when they are busy building themselves.**

*A tree does not stop growing because someone complains about its branches.*

*And a person building something meaningful does not stop because someone refuses to see the foundation.*

We stayed silent.

Not because we were defeated.

But **because we knew exactly what we were creating.**
